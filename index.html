Basketball was invented by James W. Naismith to keep his students fit during the winter. In December 1891, the physical education teacher at the YMCA International Training School in Springfield, USA sought a suitable indoor sport – and most of his rules still apply today.

ABOUT GAMES

Basketball has many intricate rules and regulations. The International Basketball Federation has done a nice job of teasingout the most basic rules and explaining them, along with defining some of the key basketball jargon.

  • Players can score 1, 2, 3 (or even 4 points) during a possession.
  • Players score 3 points for any basket made beyond the 3 point line, in bounds.
  • Players score 2 points for any basket made inside the 3 point line, in bounds.
  • Players can score 1, 2, 3 (or even 4 points) during a possession.
  • Players score 1 point for made free throws
